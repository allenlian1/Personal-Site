Euler's Pendulum

In this study, we investigated the motion of Euler’s Pendulum by developing both an experimental setup and a theoretical model to analyze its dynamics. We constructed a controlled system where a magnetic rod was suspended beneath a fixed neodymium magnet and tracked its oscillatory motion under various conditions. Using motion-tracking techniques, we collected experimental data to examine key parameters such as rod length, magnet strength, and surface thickness. To complement our observations, we formulated a theoretical model based on Lagrangian mechanics, incorporating factors like moment of inertia and magnetic interactions. By comparing experimental results with theoretical predictions, we gained deeper insights into the pendulum’s behavior, including dissipation effects and the forces driving its motion.
